## Seo Seoa Dominates: Crowned 2025 Kamui WPA Women's 9-Ball World Champion

The billiard world watched in awe as the 2025 Kamui WPA Women's 9-Ball World Championship culminated in a spectacular fashion, with South Korea's own Seo Seoa claiming the coveted title. Held as a highlight of the Predator Pro Billiard Series, this event concluded a landmark season, underscoring the incredible skill and fierce competition present in women's professional pool.

Seo Seoa's triumph isn't just a personal victory; it's a testament to the growing global presence and undeniable talent emerging from Asia, further solidifying her status as a formidable force on the international stage.

Looking ahead, the landscape of professional pool is set to undergo a significant evolution with the World Nineball Tour's exciting unveiling of its initial 2026 schedule. While specific locations and prize details are still emerging, the commitment to a comprehensive global tour demonstrates a clear vision for the sport's growth and increased professionalism. ## Inspiration on the Baize: European Billiards Champions for Athletes with Disabilities Crowned

In a truly inspiring display of dedication and talent, the European Pool Championships for Athletes with Disabilities in Starachowice, Poland, celebrated its 2025 champions. Poland's Adam Wilk and Cyprus's Georgios Malekkidis were crowned European Billiards Champions, showcasing exceptional skill and sportsmanship.

This championship, proudly recognized by both AZBilliards and the WPA, highlights the sport's unwavering commitment to inclusivity and provides a vital platform for athletes with disabilities to compete at an elite level. Their victories are not just about winning titles; they are powerful examples of perseverance, challenging perceptions and proving that the spirit of competition thrives across all abilities.
